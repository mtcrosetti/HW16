Correlations Discovered Between Smokers & Income

As the graph shows, there is a trend that people in states with lower incomes tend to take up smoking more than people in states with higher incomes.

On the high smoking end, 28% of West Virginians are smokers. Their median income was 49th out of 50. On the high end of income, Maryland (ranked #1 in median income) had about 16% of their polled population as smokers.

While the graph was mostly linear, there were some outliers - most notably California and Utah. California's lack of smokers could be due to a more healthy lifestyle, though this would require further research. Utah's lack of smoking might be due to a religious element, but this too would require further research.
